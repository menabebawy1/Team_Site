Village East Cinema Needs Your Help

The impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on small businesses threatens not only the lives of the businesses' owners and staff themselves but also the joy and fulfillment that they brought to many of their guests and customers. 48% of Americans rely on work in small businesses support their families, thus having affected and continue to affect a myriad of local businesses in the country. Village East Cinema is one of many cherished and respected historical establishments that have the full brunt of this. They need our help to stay alive and continue to serve its beloved staff, customers and their families!

Built and established in 1926 and originally called "The Louis N. Jaffe Theater", named after the Brooklyn lawyer, developer and promninent Jewish leader who built it: Louis N. Jaffe, Village East Cinema is home to premiering some of the most eclectic independent films as well the world's favorite blockbuster names. Its humble beginnings began as a house for many different Yiddish theater companies, later being used as an off-Broadway theater venue, showcasing the original productions famous plays such as Grease and Joseph & the Technicolor Dreamcoat.

Thank you for considering donating to Village East Cinema ♥
